It was two months ago, during the lockdown, when it occurred to me that – whether an event A will happen or not should have an equal probability of 0.5. Likewise, in the dice problem, if 3 will occur or not, should have the probability of 0.5 because well, it can either occur or not; but actually it is attributed a probability of 1/6 and not 0.5. This left me scratching my head for hours. It is evident here that probability depends upon the total number of equally likely outcomes. Nevertheless, I was still adamant on probability being 0.5. As I dug deeper, I realised that, not only I used to come up with just two possible outcomes every single time, but also limited myself to a probability of them being 0.5. Those outcomes were such that they didn’t even have the meaning of their own like right or wrong, yes or no, best or worst, and last but not the least, success or failure. With this point of view, we tend to close doors to the different possibilities in our life and I know, I would never have had the right answer to the dice problem if I continued with such a perspective.

So, I started learning about this measure of Uncertainty in order to open up about different possible outcomes in my life and eventually, how to approach the one that needed to be accomplished. While reading about the classification of events, I came across the most basic type of events called Simple Event. An event is said to be a simple event if it corresponds to a single outcome. For example: if we toss a coin three times successively, eight outcomes are possible which are given by the set: {HHH, HHT, HTT, TTT, TTH, THH, THT, HTH}

Let the desired outcome be getting all heads. There’s only one all heads outcome: {HHH}. So that is a simple event with probability 1/8.

Then there is another type of events called Compound Event which corresponds to more than one possible outcome. Considering the same example as we did for understanding the simple event, there are four possible outcomes with at least two heads which are: {HHH, HHT, THH, HTH}. Now that’s a compound event with a probability of 4/8 = 1/2

Clearly, the probability of a compound event (1/2), which among other outcomes, also includes our desired simple event outcome (all heads), is more than that of the simple event alone (1/8). So why not come up with a compound event that incorporates our desired outcome. Shouldn’t we initially approach things with this thought process when we want to accomplish something?

Coming back to the example, at least now we rule out rest of the four outcomes: {TTT, TTH, THT, HTT}. Out of the favourable outcomes, probability that all heads occur, is equal to 1/4. This narrowing down step towards the desired outcome by ruling out irrelevant ones, leads us to another term in probability theory, which is Conditional Probability. Conditional probability of an event is the probability of that event happening if some condition has been fulfilled. In our example, all heads are occurring only when at least two heads have occurred first. We see that this probability(1/4) is more than that of the simple event(1/8).

If we see outcomes only as success or failure, what would a non-binary way of looking at them be? Let’s enjoy more cups of tea and look outside the window to a rainy day with a better productivity at hand.

In the end I also learnt about Bernoulli Trial which is a trial having only two outcomes, usually referred to as success and failure. This brings us back to where we started this article. How I considered life to be dichotomous when there’s so much more to it than just trivial games of success versus failure. 

Perhaps, life is not a Bernoulli Trial. There is a whole lot of learning and experiencing to be done. This is, well, just the beginning.
